### Summary

Today, I learned about **Decision Tree** and **Random Forest**, two important supervised machine learning algorithms used for classification tasks. The trainer explained how Decision Trees make predictions using **if-then-else** conditions and introduced concepts such as **Gini Impurity**, **Entropy**, and **Information Gain** for selecting the best feature splits.

We implemented both Decision Tree and Random Forest models on a **Teen Mental Health Dataset** to predict depression status. The models were evaluated using **Accuracy Score**, **Classification Report**, and **Confusion Matrix**. We also visualized the Decision Tree and analyzed **Feature Importance** in the Random Forest model to identify the most influential features.

### Key Learning
- Understanding the working of Decision Tree and Random Forest.
- Learning Gini Impurity, Entropy, and Information Gain.
- Training and evaluating classification models.
- Comparing Decision Tree and Random Forest performance.
- Analyzing feature importance for better model interpretation.
